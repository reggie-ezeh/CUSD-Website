Defining the next generation of sustainable design

The Currents team is working on high-efficiency HVAC (heating, ventilation, and air conditioning) sensors using various tracking technologies and machine learning that will be implemented in Upson Hall.

Re-designing the current public transportation system in Tompkins County, NY

The Sustainable Mobility project team has been tasked by the Tompkins Consolidated Area Transit (TCAT) with re-envisioning an integrated bus system throughout the county. A mix of engineers, designers, architects, and planners have been designing bus shelters, revisiting bus routes and bus stop locations, and developing a real time bus information system interface, all informed by empathy fieldwork.

Project Lead: Joaquin Jerez | Email: jbj56@cornell.edu Learn More

Designing a two acre demonstration plot in Delhi, India

In collaboration with its stakeholder Social Awareness Newer Alternatives (SANA), Roots will focus on the development of an innovative and informative two-acre demonstration plot for small-holder farmers in India.

This exhibit will be designed to showcase cutting-edge agricultural practices that will improve crop yield while reducing waste and resource usage.

The project is currently in its research phase. Topics of study include the following: permaculture, crop rotation, hydroponics and aeroponics, vermicompost, rainwater harvesting, and sustainable irrigation methods.

Project Lead: Margot Pitney | Email: map374@cornell.edu Learn More

Alternative Recycling Cornell

Established in Fall 2017 as a project team and integrated into CUSD in Spring 2018, Alternative Recycling Cornell (ARC) will involve completing work on a plastic shredder and creating a plastic mold-injector to reshape plastic waste into other simple materials.

Eventually, we plan to introduce this alternative waste system to the local Cornell/ Ithaca community, where we can re-purpose plastic waste on a broader scale.

Additionally, ARC will consist of community outreach, educating local elementary school students and the general public about recycling.

Project Lead: Kristen Ajmo | Email: kla67@cornell.edu Learn More

Big changes, small homes

The goal of this project is to create a mobile tiny home with hands-on, interactive exhibits that show the inner workings of energy efficient features, renewable energy, and other home technologies. This tiny home will then be brought to diverse audiences throughout the area to educate them on easy, cost saving techniques they can implement in their homes.
